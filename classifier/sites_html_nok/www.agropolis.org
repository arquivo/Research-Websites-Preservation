		  <h3>The High-Tech Europe project &rsquo;European Network for integrating novel technologies for food processing&rsquo; aims to establish a network of excellence (22 partners in 10 countries). </h3>
                <h3>The funding of this project was approved in 2008.</h3>
                <p>&nbsp;</p>
                <p>The <a href="http://www.dil-ev.de/index.php?id=11&amp;L=1" target="_blank">German Institute of food technologies</a>, in Quakenbr&uuml;ck, is the coordinator of the project. </p>
                <p>Agropolis International, the <a href="http://umr-iate.cirad.fr/" target="_blank" onmouseover="tooltip.show('Website in French');" onmouseout="tooltip.hide();">UMR IATE</a> and the competitiveness cluster <a href="http://www.qualimediterranee.fr/home.html" target="_blank">Qualim&eacute;diterran&eacute;e</a> are the 3 French partners of the project.</p>
                <p>Agropolis International is involved in many work packages and manages a budget of 170K &euro;. <br />
                  Main objectives of the project:</p>
                <ul class="disque">
                <li>to identify strategic (available or forthcoming) scientific knowledge, regarding to its innovative potential for the agro-food industry                
                <li> to establish a European Institute for Food Processing (EU-IFP). This will develop the durable links between R&amp;D, industry and consumers.                
                <li> through the EU-IFP activities, to improve the European agro-food enterprises (from main firms to SME&rsquo;s) competitiveness on the international market.<br />
                    </ul>
