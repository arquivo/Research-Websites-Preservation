<p><b>Symbrion</b> (Symbiotic Evolutionary Robot Organisms) is a project funded by the <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">European Commission</a> between 2008 - 2013 to develop a framework in which a homogeneous <a href="/wiki/Swarm" title="Swarm" class="mw-redirect">swarm</a> of miniature interdependent <a href="/wiki/Robot" title="Robot">robots</a> can co-assemble into a larger robotic organism to gain problem-solving momentum.</p>
<p>One of the key aspects of Symbrion is inspired by the biological world: an artificial <a href="/wiki/Genome" title="Genome">genome</a> that allows to store and evolve (sub)optimal configurations in order to achieve an increased speed of adaptation.</p>
<p>The SYMBRION project does not start from zero; previous development and research from projects I-SWARM and the <a href="/wiki/Open-source" title="Open-source" class="mw-redirect">open-source</a> SWARMROBOT serve as a mounting point. A large part of the developments within Symbrion is open-source and open-hardware.</p>
