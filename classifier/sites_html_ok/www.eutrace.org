    <div class="field field-name-field-body field-type-text-long field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even"> <h3><strong>Final report</strong></h3>

<ul><li>The EuTRACE final report can be downloaded <a href="http://www.iass-potsdam.de/de/publikationen/projektberichte" target="_blank">here</a>.</li>
	<li><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=srrlTt-1hq8" target="_blank">Video</a> of the presentation of the report.</li>
</ul><h3><strong>Background</strong></h3>

<p>Climate Engineering (CE) is rapidly gaining scientific, political, commercial, and public attention as a possible response to climate change. The project “European Trans-disciplinary Assessment of Climate Engineering” (EuTRACE) was formed to compliment other national and international assesments of CE, in particular through providing a distinctly European perspective that draws upon contributions from a range of scientific and non-expert stakeholders.</p>

<h3><strong>Partners</strong></h3>

<p>EuTRACE brings together 14 partner organizations from Germany, the UK, France, Austria and Norway.</p>

<p>     see the full list of<a href="/partners"> EuTRACE partners</a></p>

<h3><strong>Funding</strong></h3>

<p>EuTRACE is a Coordination and Support action-project funded by the European Commission within the Seventh Framework Programme (FP7), running from 2012 - 2015. The contents of this website are the sole responsibility of EuTRACE and can in no way be taken to reflect the views of the European Union.</p>

<p>     see the <a href="http://cordis.europa.eu/project/rcn/103896_en.html">European Commision's EuTRACE page</a></p>
