	</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	<div class="entry-content">
		<p style="text-align: justify;">Resilience2050 is a  European FP7 collaborative <a href="http://cordis.europa.eu/projects/index.cfm?fuseaction=app.details&amp;TXT=resilience2050.eu&amp;FRM=1&amp;STP=10&amp;SIC=&amp;PGA=&amp;CCY=&amp;PCY=&amp;SRC=&amp;LNG=en&amp;REF=103782">research project</a> . It was launched on the 1st of June, 2012 under FP7-Transport sub-programme area AAT.2012.6.2-4. The duration of the project is 43 months. Technical and budget information can be found on <a href="http://cordis.europa.eu/projects/index.cfm?fuseaction=app.details&amp;TXT=resilience2050.eu&amp;FRM=1&amp;STP=10&amp;SIC=&amp;PGA=&amp;CCY=&amp;PCY=&amp;SRC=&amp;LNG=en&amp;REF=103782">CORDIS</a>, the EU&#8217;s Community Research and Development Information Service website.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">The main aim of this project is to achieve a deeper knowledge of the resilience concept in ATM, which today only incorporates the aspect of safety management. Resilience, which describes <strong>the ability of a system to recover</strong> under abnormal conditions to a given state is investigated thoroughly in domains with socio-ecological background. Resilience2050 will transfer this knowledge to aviation (ATM, sectors, airports) applying it as a new property of the system and use innovative <a href="http://www.complexworld.eu/datascienceworkshop/">Data Science techniques</a> to mine different datasets to extract information on how resilient to disturbances the system is. Design principles will be derived that consider only safety and physical constraints, taking into account the quantitative assessments made through the data mining process. Resilience2050 will be presenting a new concept for an efficient and resilient future ATM system. Further info can be found in <a href="http://resilience2050.innaxis.org/objectives/">Objectives</a>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Resilience2050 is coordinated by Innaxis, and there are six participants from five different countries:</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">DLR: Deutsches Zentrum für Luft- und Raumfahrt e.V., The German Aerospace Centre &#8211; Germany<br />
NLR: Nationaal Lucht- en Ruimtevaartlaboratorium, The National Aerospace Laboratory &#8211; The Netherlands<br />
ITU: Istanbul Teknik Universitesi, Istanbul Technical University &#8211; Turkey<br />
DHMI: Devlet Hava Meydanlari Isletmesi, The General Directorate of State Airports Authority of Turkey &#8211; Turkey<br />
UPM: Universidad Politécnica de Madrid &#8211; Spain<br />
KCL: King&#8217;s College London &#8211; United Kingdom</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">and</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">INX: The Innaxis Foundation and Research Institute &#8211; Spain</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Partners come mainly from the aviation field and ensure a 360 degree vision of ATM and resilience concepts. To find more information and concrete roles in the project please visit our <a href="http://resilience2050.innaxis.org/partners/">Partner page</a> .</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">All the work already <a href="http://resilience2050.innaxis.org/work-completed/">completed</a>, in addition to <a href="http://resilience2050.innaxis.org/meetings/">meetings</a> and <a href="http://resilience2050.innaxis.org/dissemination/">dissemination activities</a> can be found in this Resilience2050 site. As this is a living web, updated information will be provided as soon as possible as issues, upgrades and revisions occur during the project&#8217;s development.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">The Resilience2050 team relies on wiki tools: The project consortium uses an internal collaborative web tool affectionately named &#8216;InGrid&#8217;, which can be found <a href="https://research.innaxis.org/display/resilience/03+Project+Execution">here</a>. For all other aviation and resilience stakeholders, we have enabled the comment area in the lower part of the pages on the site. Please feel free to add comments in any of the pages and do not hesitate to contact us should you want to be included in communications from Resilience2050 and punctually informed of upcoming Resilience2050 events.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">The Resilience2050 team welcomes you and hopes you enjoy our project website!</p>
