<h3>The development of a single-screw extrusion process for the
production of low density biodegradable PLA foam for thermoformed food
packaging applications</h3>

<p>&nbsp;<img class="image-inline image-inline" src="http://pla-foam.uk-matri.org/copy_of_CIMG0045.JPG/image_preview" alt="PLA Foam Extruder" height="434" width="525" align="left" hspace="12" /></p>

<p>The use of polymers for food packing is an essential part of the
food production and transportation process to reduce food damage and
spoilage, reduce the risk of contamination and increase product
lifetimes. Currently within the EU, ~22 million tonnes of polymers are
used for packaging applications annually. Of these 450,000 tonnes p.a.
are foamed polymers which are manufactured from synthetic polymers such
as Expanded Polystyrene (EPS) of density ~50 kg/m. However, foamed
polymers are difficult to recycle cost-effectively and as a result only
a very small proportion are recycled at the end of life, with the vast
majority going to landfill.</p>

<p>As a result of these considerable issues, the market place is
looking for a soluion where by these products can be composted at the
end of life or will degrade in the environment if accidentally released
as litter. Of the renewable polymers being commercialised, only Poly
Lactic Acid (PLA) is produced in sufficient quantities to be a
commercial solution.</p>

<p>PLA Foam looks to develop&nbsp;a single screw extruder capable of
foaming PLA to a level suitable for thermoforming.</p>

<p>&nbsp;</p>

<p style="text-align: left;"><strong>The research leading to these
results has received funding from the European Union&nbsp;Seventh
Framework Programme (</strong><strong><em>FP7-SME-2008-1</em>) under
<em>grant agreement</em> n° 232145</strong></p>

<p style="text-align: left;"><img class="image-inline" src="http://pla-foam.uk-matri.org/PLA_FP7_EU_Flag.jpg/image_preview" alt="PLA_Foam_Eu_FP7_Flag" height="91" width="400" /></p>
