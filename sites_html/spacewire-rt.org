<h1>Home</h1>

<p>
With the growing trend towards “Operationally Responsive Space”, demand is&nbsp;increasing for more flexible spacecraft that can be&nbsp;assembled to&nbsp;meet a&nbsp;specific mission requirement and launched quickly. The SpW-RT project is&nbsp;responding to&nbsp;this demand, paving the way for enhanced cooperation between the&nbsp;EU and Russia in&nbsp;this exciting technology field. 
</p><p>
The success of&nbsp;future space missions is&nbsp;linked closely to&nbsp;our ability to&nbsp;design spacecraft that are both flexible and able to&nbsp;be&nbsp;assembled rapidly to&nbsp;meet specific customer demands. Flexibility in&nbsp;this respect is&nbsp;a&nbsp;synonym for the ability to&nbsp;build spacecraft that can be&nbsp;adapted from one mission to&nbsp;another, in&nbsp;order to&nbsp;respond to&nbsp;mission specific needs. Rapid assembly implies fast integration of&nbsp;pre-build components that can be&nbsp;configured to&nbsp;meet a&nbsp;wide range of&nbsp;space mission requirements. 
</p><p>
Striving to&nbsp;achieve more, rapidly and at&nbsp;lower cost, spacecraft designers see a&nbsp;need for enhancing the degree of&nbsp;joint technology standards between space faring nations. 
</p><p>
The SpW-RT project is&nbsp;set to&nbsp;promote such interoperability between&nbsp;EU and Russian space technologies. The project aims to&nbsp;provide a&nbsp;flexible, robust, responsive, deterministic and durable standard network technology for spacecraft avionics, i.e. spacecraft onboard data-handling and control electronics. 
</p><p>
SpW-RT will improve the existing SpaceWire protocol in&nbsp;a&nbsp;number of&nbsp;ways, such as&nbsp;adding a&nbsp;quality of&nbsp;service layer. Such developments may pave the way for a&nbsp;new EU-Russia standard in&nbsp;spacecraft plug-and-play network technologies, thereby creating a&nbsp;strong technical platform for further joint advances at&nbsp;the payloads and avionics level.
</p>
