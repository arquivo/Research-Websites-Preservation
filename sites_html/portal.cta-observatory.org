<h1 class="ms-rteElement-H1"><strong><span class="ms-rteThemeForeColor-4-4">Cherenkov Telescope Array</span><span></span></strong><strong><span class="ms-rteThemeForeColor-4-4"><a href="https://www.youtube.com/channel/UC0IHTTfgiiyCFLp-SH8egMw"><img class="ms-rtePosition-2" src="/PublishingImages/Social%20Media/youtube.png" alt="" style="margin:5px;width:30px;height:30px" /></a><a href="https://twitter.com/CTA_Observatory"><img class="ms-rtePosition-2" src="/PublishingImages/Social%20Media/twitter.png" alt="" style="margin:5px;width:30px;height:30px" /></a><a href="https://www.facebook.com/ctaobservatory"><img class="ms-rtePosition-2" src="/PublishingImages/Social%20Media/facebook.png" alt="" style="margin:5px;width:30px;height:30px" /></a><br /></span></strong></h1>

<h2 class="ms-rteElement-H2 ms-rteThemeForeColor-2-0"><div>An observatory for ground-based gamma-ray astronomy.<img class="ms-rtePosition-2" src="/SiteAssets/Pages/Home/CTA_array_sm.jpg" alt="CTA_array_sm.jpg" style="margin:35px 5px" /><br />​<br /></div></h2>


<span class="ms-rteFontSize-2 ms-rteThemeForeColor-2-0"></span><div class="ms-rteFontSize-2 ms-rteThemeForeColor-2-0"><p>The CTA project is an initiative to build the next generation
 ground-based very high energy gamma-ray instrument. It will serve as an
 open observatory to a wide astrophysics community and will provide a 
deep insight into the non-thermal high-energy universe. A short movie 
outlining the envisaged arrays is available <a href="/Pages/Instrument.aspx">here</a>. A special edition of the journal Astroparticle Physics with a focus on CTA can be accessed <a href="http://www.sciencedirect.com/science/journal/09276505/43">here</a>.</p>
<p><span>The aims of the CTA can be roughly grouped into three main themes, serving as key science drivers:
</span></p>
<ol><li>Understanding the origin of cosmic rays and their role in the Universe</li>
<li>Understanding the nature and variety of particle acceleration around black holes</li>
<li>Searching for the ultimate nature of matter and physics beyond the Standard Model<span style="font-size:8pt"> </span><span style="font-size:8pt">​​<span style="display:inline-block"></span></span></li></ol>
The present generation of imaging atmospheric Cherenkov telescopes (<a href="http://www.mpi-hd.mpg.de/hfm/HESS/" target="_blank">H.E.S.S.</a>, <a href="http://magic.mppmu.mpg.de/" target="_blank">MAGIC</a> and <a href="http://veritas.sao.arizona.edu/" target="_blank">VERITAS</a>) has in recent years opened the realm of ground-based gamma ray astronomy
 in the energy range above a few tens of GeV. The Cherenkov Telescope 
Array (CTA) will explore our Universe in depth in Very High Energy 
(VHE,  E &gt; 10 GeV) gamma-rays and investigate cosmic non-thermal 
processes, in close cooperation with observatories operating at other 
wavelength ranges of the electromagnetic spectrum, and those using other messengers such as cosmic rays and neutrinos.<br />
 <br />
Besides anticipated high-energy astrophysics results, CTA will have a 
large discovery potential in key areas of astronomy, astrophysics and 
fundamental physics research. These include the study of the origin of 
cosmic rays and their impact on the constituents of the Universe, the 
investigation of the nature and variety of black hole particle 
accelerators, and the inquiry into the ultimate nature of matter and 
physics beyond the Standard Model, searching for dark matter and effects
 of quantum gravity.<p></p>

<p>The design foresees a factor of 5-10 improvement in sensitivity in 
the current very high energy gamma ray domain of about 100 GeV to some 
10 TeV, <span class="ms-rteThemeFontFace-1 ms-rteFontSize-2 ms-rteThemeForeColor-2-0">and an extension of the accessible energy ra</span><span class="ms-rteThemeFontFace-1 ms-rteFontSize-2 ms-rteThemeForeColor-2-0">nge from well below 
100 GeV to above 100 TeV.</span></p>
