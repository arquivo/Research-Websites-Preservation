<tr><td colspan='2'><b>GEFOS</b> stands for the GEnetic Factors for OSteoporosis Consortium. It is a large international collaboration involving various prominent research groups.</td><tr>

<table>
<tr><td width='95%'>Osteoporosis is a common age-related complex disease with a strong genetic component. Osteoporotic fractures account for considerable disease burden and costs. The <b>genes responsible</b>, however, are poorly defined. It is by now generally assumed that - like for many other complex diseases such as diabetes and cardiovascular disease - many gene variants are responsible but each with <b>subtle effect</b>. In a previous EU FP5 funded project, named <b>GENOMOS</b>, we have improved on this situation by bringing together several of Europe's largest collections of osteoporosic study populations with DNA available, and analysing the most commonly studied. </td><td width='5%'><img src='img/hip.png' align='right'></td><tr>
</table>

<tr><td width='5%'><img src='img/chips.png' /></td><td width='95%'>With the GEFOS project we here propose to capitalize on the success of GENOMOS by using the most advanced gene discovery tools that have become recently available, i.e., <b>Genome Wide Association</b> (GWA) analysis with high density SNP arrays, to identify common risk gene variants for osteoporosis. </td><tr>
</table>
